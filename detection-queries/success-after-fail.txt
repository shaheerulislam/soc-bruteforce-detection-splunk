Detection Name: Successful Login After Multiple Failed Attempts

SOC Context:
This detection identifies a successful authentication following multiple failed login attempts, which may indicate a compromised account after a brute-force attack.

Why This Matters:
Attackers often succeed after repeated password guessing attempts. Detecting successful login after failures helps identify credential compromise.

Detection Logic (Splunk Query):

index=auth_logs sourcetype=linux_secure
("Failed password" OR "Accepted password")
| stats count by src_ip, user, message

SOC Analyst Actions:
- Verify legitimacy of login activity
- Compare IP address history and reputation
- Check time correlation between failed and successful logins
- Escalate if abnormal login pattern is confirmed

Severity Guidance:
- Medium: Single user affected
- High: Successful login after high number of failures

SOC Value:
Demonstrates behavioural correlation analysis and post-compromise detection capability.
